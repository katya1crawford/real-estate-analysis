Lender Required Property Appraisal

You need to monitor and talk to the appraiser to make sure: he has the proper information such as the most up to date rent roll; pending transactions, the most recent lease and sale comparables, any additional income that the property is generating and anything else that will enhance the value of the property.

Although many of the appraisers do not wish to speak with the borrower, it is necessary to make that initial contact with them to make sure that you’re not going to get "short-changed" on the appraisal, because they weren’t provided all they could have been to increase the value of the appraisal.

If you are not being proactive with the appraiser and let the appraisal process go on "auto-pilot", you run a high chance of getting a lower appraisal and therefore less desirable loan amount and potentially higher down-payment amount.

Appraisers incorporate unverified secondhand data, appraisers often err in fact and interpretation. Accept an appraisal report as for-what-it’s-worth information. Always verify the appraiser’s comp property data before accepting it.
