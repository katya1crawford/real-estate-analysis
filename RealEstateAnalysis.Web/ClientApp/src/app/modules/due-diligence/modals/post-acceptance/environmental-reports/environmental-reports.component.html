Environmental Reports Phase I, II & III

If available, also obtain environmental report(s) that was done when current owner purchased the property.

Make sure report for environmental issues such as asbestos, mold, lead paint, or radon is done as well.

  • Phase I: Reports are basic reports that are done by physical inspection of the property, as well are review of historical records and aerials to determine if there are any environmental concerns or issues; if so, what recommendations are made? There can include lab samples collected to determine if asbestos or other contaminants are present.
  • Phase II: Reports are done because of the recommendations made in the Phase I Report to further determine the extent of any suspected concerns, issues or foundings. Also, ground borings could be recommended to pull oil samples, if there are suspected ground contaminants such as gasoline, petroleum products or tetracycline from previous use of the property, for example, a gas station or dry cleaners.
  • Phase III: Reports are the recommendations for the remediation and removal of any contaminants found as a result of conducting the Phase II investigation.

Health, safety, and environmental violations may present these risks:

  • Subject you tenants to injury.
  • Motivate a rent strike.
  • Expose you to a lawsuit.
  • Expose you to civil or criminal penalties (fines and, in serious cases, prison).
