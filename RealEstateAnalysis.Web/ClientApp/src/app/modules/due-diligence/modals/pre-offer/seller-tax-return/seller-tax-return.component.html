Seller’s Tax Return - IRS Schedule E

One of the most powerful tools you cn use to determine the truth about a property’s financials is the seller’s tax return. A seller will not likely make a property look extra "cash flow-push" for the IRS, so tax returns will likely be the most accurate representation of how the property really performs.

To get it - simply ask for it. Also, talk to CPA to find out if you can get it directly from IRS.

Look for any discrepancies between tax returns filed and the financial information provided by the seller. If you find inconsistencies between the numbers they provide and the numbers on the tax return, dig further into this.
