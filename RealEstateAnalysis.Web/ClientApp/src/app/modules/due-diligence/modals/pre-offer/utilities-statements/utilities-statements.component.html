Utilities Statements

Utilities are a major expense with real estate, so verify that the numbers you used for your math homework are accurate. Find out and verify who pays for which utility. Call utility companies and get copies of most recent statements from all utility companies that landlord is responsible for paying.

Get contact information of all utility companies that tenants are responsible for paying.

Verify all the landlord responsible utility bills by calling each utility company and getting the last twelve months operating history on each account. Get estimate of the rates for the next year. Insert revised figures into your budget. Always check to make sure the utility bills have all been paid.

Energy Audits

Nearly all utility companies will show you how to reduce your gas or electric bills. Some will perform an energy audit and inspection of your property. Google "energy audit" or "energy savings" to get ideas.

Conduct an energy audit on a building before you buy it. Judge beforehand the extent to which you can feasibly lower these costs. Avoid energy-hog buildings for which no solution exists.
